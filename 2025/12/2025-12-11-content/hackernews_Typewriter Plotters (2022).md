# Typewriter Plotters

Posted on [December 5, 2022](https://biosrhythm.com/?p=2143 "5:11 pm") by [paulrickards](https://biosrhythm.com/?author=1 "View all posts by paulrickards")

Did you know there were typewriters that used ball point pens to draw not just text but also graphics? I‚Äôve collected several of these over the years. Read on to discover a world that you didn‚Äôt know existed.

* [![](https://biosrhythm.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/1482114631913115650-FJGHZcTXsAIsyUQ-600x800.jpg)](https://biosrhythm.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/1482114631913115650-FJGHZcTXsAIsyUQ.jpg)
* [![](https://biosrhythm.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/1482114631913115650-FJGHZdzXoAQUuKw-600x800.jpg)](https://biosrhythm.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/1482114631913115650-FJGHZdzXoAQUuKw.jpg)

Typewriter plotters could act as a normal typewriter in that you could type on the keys and have the text printed on the page. The difference is it would use a tiny ball point pen to ‚Äúdraw‚Äù the text, character by character, onto the page. It‚Äôs mesmerizing to watch! Many also included the ability to print graphs and bar charts, although in practice is was likely cumbersome. In addition, some models had the ability to connect to a computer to print text or even custom graphics.

## Panasonic **RK-P400C Penwriter**

Panasonic made three models. The top shelf was the **RK-P400C Penwriter** which included the RS-232 port built in for computer control. They also came with a white pen for error correcting.

## Panasonic RK-P440 Penwriter

A lower end model was the Panasonic RK-P440 Penwriter. It had a computer input but required the K100 external interface. Otherwise functionally the same: draws texts as well as business graphics with 4 color ballpoint pens. Portable using 6 C batteries.

* [![](https://biosrhythm.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/1231591753158995969-ERd-Ky0XYAA4SsV-840x630.jpg)](https://biosrhythm.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/1231591753158995969-ERd-Ky0XYAA4SsV.jpg)

## Music Keyboard

Yamaha added a pen plotter to one of their music keyboards, the Yamaha MP-1. The idea was you‚Äôd compose music on the keyboard and it would plot the notes on paper as you played. The reality is the plotter was so much slower than you could play, it would take forever to catch up. It also wasn‚Äôt great at quantization so the notes were likely not what you‚Äôd expect.

## Pens

All of the typewriter models used the same ball point pens in four colors (black, red, green, blue) and were portable with a built-in handle and could run on batteries. They also likely all used the same plotter mechanisms made by Alps.

* [![](https://biosrhythm.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/1482114727882993672-FJGHezwXsAANqfm-840x630.jpg)](https://biosrhythm.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/1482114727882993672-FJGHezwXsAANqfm.jpg)
* [![](https://biosrhythm.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/1482114727882993672-FJGHe5NWUAkEPiW-600x800.jpg)](https://biosrhythm.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/1482114727882993672-FJGHe5NWUAkEPiW.jpg)

The pens are rather scarce now, mostly all that remains are NOS (new old stock) with some exceptions for a couple of German companies that make replacements for medical equipment that fit.

* [![](https://biosrhythm.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/1482114734883278850-FJGHfwwXMAIT70Y.jpg)](https://biosrhythm.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/1482114734883278850-FJGHfwwXMAIT70Y.jpg)

These pen typewriters were sold during the mid 1980s. In PC World magazine July 1985, the Panasonic RK-P400C retailed for $350.
